3. Concepts Used
3.1 Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Natural Language Processing is a branch of artificial intelligence that enables computers to understand, interpret, and process human language in a meaningful way.

### 3.2 Tokenization

Tokenization is the process of breaking text into smaller units such as words or sentences.

### 3.3 Stop Words

Stop words are commonly used words (such as is, the, and) that do not add significant meaning and are usually removed during analysis.

### 3.4 Stemming

Stemming reduces words to their root form by removing suffixes.

### 3.5 Lemmatization

Lemmatization converts words into their base or dictionary form using linguistic rules.

### 3.6 Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging

POS tagging assigns grammatical labels such as noun, verb, adjective, etc., to each word in a sentence.

### 3.7 Frequency Distribution

Frequency distribution counts how often each word appears in a text dataset.

### 4. Theory
4.1 Introduction to Natural Language Processing

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is widely used in modern applications such as chatbots, search engines, speech recognition systems, and text analytics. Since human language is inherently unstructured and complex, NLP techniques are used to convert it into a structured format that computers can process efficiently.

### 4.2 Tokenization

Tokenization is the first step in text processing, where text is divided into smaller components.

### Types of Tokenization:
Word Tokenization: Splits text into individual words
Sentence Tokenization: Splits text into sentences
Importance:
Simplifies complex text
Forms the foundation for further NLP tasks
Enables easier analysis and processing
4.3 Stop Word Removal

Stop words are high-frequency words that carry minimal semantic value.

Examples:

is, the, in, on, at

### Importance:
Reduces dataset size
Improves processing efficiency
Focuses analysis on meaningful words
4.4 Stemming

Stemming is the process of reducing words to their root form by removing suffixes.

### Examples:
Playing → Play
Studies → Studi
Characteristics:
Fast and simple
May produce non-dictionary words
Useful for quick text processing
4.5 Lemmatization

Lemmatization converts words into their correct base form using vocabulary and grammar rules.

Examples:
Running → Run
Better → Good
Characteristics:
Produces meaningful words
More accurate than stemming
Requires more processing time
4.6 Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging

POS tagging identifies the grammatical role of each word in a sentence.

### Common Tags:
Noun (NN)
Proper Noun (NNP)
Verb (VB)
Adjective (JJ)
Determiner (DT)
Importance:
Helps in understanding sentence structure
Useful in grammar analysis and language modeling
Assists in advanced NLP tasks
4.7 Frequency Distribution

Frequency distribution determines how often each word appears in a text.

### Importance:
Identifies commonly used or important words
Useful in keyword extraction
Helps in summarization and text analysis
4.8 Importance of NLP Techniques
Converts unstructured text into structured data
Enables machines to understand human language
Improves text analysis and information retrieval
Widely used in chatbots, translators, and recommendation systems
Supports sentiment analysis and document classification
